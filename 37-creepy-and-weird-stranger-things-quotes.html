    People love quotes from Stranger Things for a variety of reasons. First, the show features relatable and memorable characters. Many viewers see aspects of themselves or their friends reflected in characters like Eleven, Dustin, and even the uncompromisingly fierce Nancy Wheeler.

    Additionally, Stranger Things takes place in the 1980s and often references pop culture from that era. These references evoke a sense of nostalgia for those who grew up during that time period and also give younger viewers a glimpse into the past.

    Finally, the quotes from the show tend to be clever and thought-provoking. Lines like “Friends don’t lie” and “I am not your lab rat” have become popular catchphrases in everyday conversation.

    Overall, the charming characters, nostalgic setting, and quotable dialogue make Stranger Things a hit with audiences. It’s no wonder people can’t get enough of the show’s memorable quotes.

    Fun Facts About Stranger Things

    In addition to being a highly successful show loaded with memorable quotes and moments, there are also plenty of secrets and interesting facts about Stranger Things as well. Here are just a few of them.

    1. Did you know that the show was originally titled Montauk? It was changed to Stranger Things after the creators found out about another project with a similar title.

    2. The town of Hawkins, where most of the show takes place, is named after Back to the Future actor Matthew Lawrence’s mother, Gloria Hawkins.

    3. The Duffer Brothers, who created and directed the show, were inspired by classic 80s films such as E.T., Stand By Me, and The Goonies.

    4. Millie Bobby Brown (Eleven) shaved her head for the role and had to wear a wig until she filmed all of Eleven’s scenes with short hair.

    5. Gaten Matarazzo (Dustin) and Caleb McLaughlin (Lucas) both auditioned for the role of Mike.

    6. Winona Ryder, who plays Joyce Byers, improvised her iconic “I’ve got work to do” line in the season one finale.

    7. The Demogorgon, the monster in the first season, was originally called “The Monster” or “The Beast.” It was only given the name Demogorgon after fans noticed a Dungeons and Dragons poster with a similar looking monster on it in Dustin’s room.

    8. Eleven’s signature outfit of a pink dress and white sneakers was inspired by Drew Barrymore’s character in E.T.

    9. Shannon Purser, who plays fan-favorite character Barbara “Barb” Holland, only had one line in the first episode. She went on to become such a beloved character that fans started the hashtag “#JusticeForBarb.”

    10. The show’s theme song is a synth-heavy cover of The Clash’s “Should I Stay or Should I Go.” It was created by Kyle Dixon and Michael Stein, members of the band Survive.
